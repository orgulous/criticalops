About the Event

24 Hours.
One Mission.

The National Security Hackathon brings together students from Johns Hopkins, Georgetown, and George Washington University to build technology solutions for America's most pressing security challenges.

Mission

Why This Exists

The United States faces an unprecedented array of technology-driven national security threats. From adversarial AI to cyber warfare, from supply chain vulnerabilities to space domain awareness — the challenges are growing faster than the workforce to address them.

This hackathon exists to bridge that gap. We bring together the next generation of engineers, policy thinkers, and entrepreneurs from DC's top universities to prototype solutions that matter.

Inspired by the growing defense tech movement and events like the HBS-MIT National Security Conference, we believe that building — not just discussing — is the fastest path to impact.

Whether you're a CS major, an IR student, or a policy wonk who can code, there's a place for you here. The best national security solutions come from interdisciplinary teams.

FAQ

Questions

Who can participate?

Currently enrolled students at Johns Hopkins University, Georgetown University, and George Washington University. All majors and degree levels welcome — we need engineers, policy experts, designers, and domain specialists.

Do I need a team?

You can register as an individual or with a team of up to 4. We'll have team formation activities at the start for individuals. Cross-university teams are encouraged.

What should I bring?

Your laptop, charger, and any hardware you want to hack with. We provide WiFi, power, food, drinks, and API credits. A government ID for check-in.

Do I need security clearance?

No. All challenge tracks use unclassified, publicly available data and open-source tools. No clearance required.

Is it free?

Yes. Registration, food, swag, and API credits are all free thanks to our sponsors.

What about intellectual property?

You own everything you build. We encourage open-sourcing your projects but it's not required.
